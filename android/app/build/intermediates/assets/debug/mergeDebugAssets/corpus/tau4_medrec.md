# τ4 Medication Reconciliation + Interactions + Beers + Renal

Sources: 2023 AGS Beers Criteria, DailyMed, WHO Essential Medicines, Lexicomp-style concise. CDS only.

---

## med_warfarin_abx
**Warfarin + antibiotics**: TMP-SMX, ciprofloxacin, metronidazole, and azoles amplify INR via CYP2C9 inhibition and gut flora disruption. Bleeding risk up to 3× baseline. Action: check INR 48–72h after abx start, reduce warfarin ~25–50% preemptively if starting TMP-SMX or metronidazole, consider alternative abx where possible (doxycycline, cephalexin, nitrofurantoin lower interaction). Source: AGS Beers + Lexicomp Level X warnings.

---

## med_metformin_contrast
**Metformin + IV iodinated contrast**: risk of contrast-induced nephropathy → lactic acidosis. Hold metformin day of contrast and for 48h after. **Resume only after confirmed normal renal function (Cr / eGFR return to baseline).** If eGFR <60 pre-contrast → hold and consult. If emergency contrast needed in renal patient: supplemental hydration + NAC not proven. Alternative: MR contrast without iodine if available.

---

## med_cipro_qt
**Ciprofloxacin + QT-prolonging agents**: fluoroquinolones prolong QT. Combined risk with amiodarone, sotalol, dofetilide, methadone, ondansetron, haloperidol, TCAs, macrolides. Torsades de pointes risk, especially with hypokalemia / hypomagnesemia. Action: baseline ECG before ciprofloxacin if on any QT-prolonging drug, correct electrolytes (K>4, Mg>2), consider alternative abx (doxycycline, Bactrim if compatible). Avoid combining with Class III antiarrhythmics.

---

## med_ssri_tramadol
**SSRI / SNRI + tramadol / triptans / MAOI / linezolid**: serotonin syndrome risk — autonomic instability, clonus, hyperreflexia, hyperthermia, altered mental status. Can be fatal. Action: avoid combination; if unavoidable, use lowest effective dose and monitor closely. Wait 2 weeks between MAOI and SSRI (5 weeks for fluoxetine). Tramadol is particularly insidious — often prescribed for pain in depression patients.

---

## med_acei_kspare
**ACE-inhibitor / ARB + potassium-sparing diuretic**: hyperkalemia risk, can precipitate arrhythmia. Combinations: lisinopril/losartan + spironolactone / eplerenone / amiloride / triamterene. Also watch ACE + K supplement + NSAID ("triple whammy"). Action: check baseline K and Cr, recheck 1–2 weeks after initiation. Hold if K>5.5. Use spironolactone cautiously in HFrEF (beneficial at 25–50 mg but monitor).

---

## med_beers_elderly
**AGS Beers Criteria — high-risk meds in elderly (≥65)**: avoid first-gen antihistamines (diphenhydramine, hydroxyzine) — anticholinergic, falls. Avoid long-acting benzos (diazepam, chlordiazepoxide) — falls, cognitive impairment; short-acting (lorazepam, oxazepam) preferred at low dose. Avoid muscle relaxants (cyclobenzaprine, methocarbamol) — sedation, falls. Avoid NSAIDs if CKD / HF / anticoag. Avoid sliding-scale-only insulin without basal. Avoid PPI >8 weeks without indication (fractures, C diff).

## med_renal_dosing
**Renal dose adjustment — common meds**:
- **Metformin**: hold if eGFR <30; use caution 30–45.
- **Gabapentin**: 300 mg BID–TID if CrCl 30–60; 200 mg qhs if <15.
- **Enoxaparin (prophylactic)**: 30 mg q24 if CrCl <30 (not 40 q24). Therapeutic: 1 mg/kg q24 if CrCl <30.
- **Vancomycin**: target trough 15–20 for deep infections; AUC 400–600. Adjust by nephrology nomogram if CrCl <50.
- **Nitrofurantoin**: avoid if CrCl <30 (reduced efficacy + toxicity).
- **NSAIDs**: avoid in CKD stage 3+.
- **DOACs**: apixaban preferred in CKD stage 4 (2.5 mg BID if 2 of: age ≥80, weight ≤60, Cr ≥1.5); avoid dabigatran and edoxaban in CrCl <30.

## med_asa_missing_acs
**Missing aspirin in suspected ACS**: aspirin 162–325 mg chewed is a Class I indication for suspected ACS / STEMI. Absolute contraindications rare: active bleed, severe asthma w/ prior ASA-induced bronchospasm, known allergy. If clinician has not administered aspirin in an ACS-suspect encounter → flag as missing guideline-directed therapy.

---

## med_anticoag_bleeding
**Anticoagulation + bleeding risk markers**: active peptic ulcer, recent GI bleed (<6m), platelets <50, uncontrolled HTN >180/110, falls (HAS-BLED ≥3), recent intracranial bleed, active malignancy with invasive procedures. **Reassess HAS-BLED at each encounter.** Reversal: warfarin → vitamin K + PCC; DOAC → specific reversal (idarucizumab for dabigatran, andexanet for Xa inhibitors) OR PCC if unavailable.

---

## med_opioid_red_flags
**Opioid prescribing red flags**: concurrent benzodiazepine (respiratory depression, 10× overdose death), concurrent gabapentinoids, MME >50/day (CDC 2022 guideline), untreated OSA, CKD (morphine / codeine metabolites accumulate — prefer hydromorphone or fentanyl), hx SUD. Always co-prescribe naloxone if MME ≥50 or benzo concurrent. Check state PDMP.

---

## med_pregnancy_teratogens
**Teratogenic meds to screen in repro-age female**: ACEi/ARB (renal dysgenesis), warfarin (embryopathy), isotretinoin (category X), valproate (neural tube + behavioral), methotrexate, statins (avoid), lithium (Ebstein anomaly), tetracyclines after 15 weeks, fluoroquinolones (avoid if alternative), topiramate (cleft). Verify pregnancy status + contraception counseling at visit.
