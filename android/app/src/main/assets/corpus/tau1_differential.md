# τ1 Differential + Must-Rule-Outs

Sources: ACEP Clinical Policy (Acute Chest Pain), Tintinalli's Emergency Medicine 9e (condensed), WHO IMCI. Informational / CDS only.

---

## ddx_acs
**Acute coronary syndrome (ACS / MI)** — chest pain or pressure lasting >10 min, often substernal, radiates to left arm / jaw / back. Associated: diaphoresis, nausea, dyspnea, syncope. Risk factors: age >45 M / >55 F, DM, HTN, smoking, dyslipidemia, family history, prior CAD. **Must rule out first.** Immediate: 12-lead ECG within 10 min, serial troponins q3h, aspirin 325 mg chewed unless contraindicated, oxygen if SpO2 <94%, nitroglycerin if no RV infarct or PDE5 use. Source: ACEP Acute Chest Pain Clinical Policy.

---

## ddx_aortic_dissection
**Aortic dissection** — sudden tearing / ripping pain, radiating to back between scapulae. BP differential >20 mmHg between arms, pulse deficit, new aortic regurgitation murmur, widened mediastinum on CXR. Risk: uncontrolled HTN, Marfan / Loeys-Dietz / Ehlers-Danlos, pregnancy third trimester, cocaine, bicuspid aortic valve. **Avoid thrombolytics if suspected.** Confirm with CT angio aorta. Immediate: control HR <60 (esmolol / labetalol) then BP <120 systolic.

---

## ddx_pe
**Pulmonary embolism** — pleuritic chest pain, dyspnea, tachycardia, sometimes hemoptysis. Unilateral leg swelling / calf tenderness = DVT. Risk: recent surgery / immobility / long flight, OCP / HRT / pregnancy, active malignancy, prior VTE, thrombophilia. Wells score: >6 high, 2–6 moderate, <2 low + PERC rules out if all negative. Confirm: CT pulmonary angiography or V/Q if renal fail / contrast allergy. Treat: anticoagulation + thrombolytics if massive (hemodynamic instability).

---

## ddx_tension_ptx
**Tension pneumothorax** — sudden severe chest pain + dyspnea, tracheal deviation away from affected side, absent / decreased breath sounds, hyperresonance, hypotension, JVD, cyanosis. **Clinical diagnosis — do NOT wait for CXR.** Immediate needle decompression: 14G at 2nd intercostal space mid-clavicular OR 4th/5th intercostal anterior axillary. Follow with tube thoracostomy.

---

## ddx_esophageal_rupture
**Esophageal rupture (Boerhaave syndrome)** — severe retrosternal chest or epigastric pain immediately after forceful vomiting. Subcutaneous emphysema (crepitus on neck / chest), Hamman's sign (crunch on auscultation), mediastinal air on CXR, pleural effusion. Confirm with gastrografin contrast esophagram or CT. Mortality high if >24h delay. Surgical consult STAT + broad-spectrum abx + NPO.

---

## ddx_pericarditis_tamponade
**Pericarditis / cardiac tamponade** — sharp positional chest pain worse lying flat, relieved leaning forward. Pericardial friction rub. ECG: diffuse ST elevation with PR depression (pericarditis). Tamponade = Beck's triad: hypotension, JVD, muffled heart sounds + pulsus paradoxus. Echo confirms effusion. Tamponade = urgent pericardiocentesis.

---

## ddx_gerd_musculoskeletal
**GERD / costochondritis** (diagnoses of exclusion only after ACS/PE/dissection ruled out) — retrosternal burning relieved by antacids, reproducible chest wall tenderness on palpation (costochondritis), no exertional component. **Do NOT anchor to these until serious causes excluded.**

---

## ddx_peds_fever_high_risk
**Pediatric fever — high-risk groups** (WHO IMCI). Age <3 months with any fever (rectal ≥38°C) → full sepsis workup regardless of appearance (blood + urine + CSF, empiric abx). Age 3–36 months: toxic appearance, petechial rash, bulging fontanelle, stiff neck → LP + meningitis coverage. Tachypnea RR>60 infants / >40 toddlers = pneumonia / sepsis. SpO2 <92% → admit. Poor feeding / lethargy / capillary refill >3s = compensated shock.

---

## ddx_polypharmacy_geriatric
**Geriatric polypharmacy encounter** — always ask: how many prescribers? who filled when? any recent hospital discharge? over-the-counter / supplements? Beers Criteria screens: anticholinergic load, long-acting benzo, NSAIDs if CKD, first-gen antihistamines, muscle relaxants. STOPP/START framework: check for under-prescribing (statin post-MI, anticoag post-stroke, ACEi in HF). Red flag combos: warfarin + new antibiotic, metformin + contrast, SSRI + tramadol.

---

## ddx_stroke_lvo
**Large-vessel occlusion stroke** — sudden focal neuro deficit. FAST: Face droop, Arm weakness, Speech slurred, Time to call. NIHSS ≥6 suggests LVO. Time windows: tPA <4.5h from last known well (strict contraindications), mechanical thrombectomy <24h if LVO on CTA. Immediate: non-contrast CT to rule out hemorrhage, then CTA head+neck, glucose, BP control (target <185/110 pre-tPA).

---

## ddx_sepsis
**Sepsis / septic shock** — suspected infection + qSOFA ≥2 (SBP<100, RR≥22, altered mental status) OR SIRS ≥2 (fever, tach, tachypnea, WBC shift). Septic shock = persistent hypotension requiring pressors after 30 mL/kg fluid + lactate >2. Hour-1 bundle: lactate, blood cx × 2, broad-spectrum abx, 30 mL/kg crystalloid, reassess, initiate pressors if MAP <65.
