# τ3 Red-Flag Alerts

Sources: Surviving Sepsis Campaign 2021, AHA/ACC STEMI, AHA Stroke Guidelines, ACEP Hypertensive Emergency, WHO IMCI Danger Signs.

---

## rf_sepsis_bundle
**SEPSIS RED FLAG.** Trigger: infection suspicion + ≥2 of (T<36 or >38.3°C, HR>90, RR>20, WBC<4 or >12, altered mental status). Septic shock = hypotension SBP<90 after 30 mL/kg fluid + lactate >2. Hour-1 bundle: (1) lactate; (2) blood cultures × 2 BEFORE abx; (3) broad-spectrum empiric abx; (4) 30 mL/kg crystalloid if lactate ≥4 or hypotensive; (5) vasopressors (norepi first-line) if MAP <65 post-fluid. Source MIR: SSC 2021.

---

## rf_stemi
**STEMI RED FLAG.** Criteria: ≥1 mm ST elevation in 2 contiguous leads OR new LBBB with Sgarbossa criteria OR posterior STEMI (reciprocal depression V1-V3). Door-to-balloon <90 min (PCI) or door-to-needle <30 min (fibrinolytics if PCI delayed >120 min). Immediate meds: aspirin 325 mg chewed, P2Y12 (ticagrelor/clopidogrel), heparin / LMWH, high-intensity statin. Avoid NG if RV infarct (inferior MI + RV leads) or PDE5 use within 24h.

---

## rf_stroke_lvo
**STROKE / LVO RED FLAG.** FAST positive + time of last known well. tPA window <4.5h (strict exclusions: recent surgery, anticoag, prior ICH, active bleed, BP >185/110). Thrombectomy window <24h if proximal LVO on CTA. Door-to-CT <25 min. BP target pre-tPA: <185/110 (labetalol or nicardipine). Glucose check — exclude hypoglycemia mimic. NIHSS, CT head non-contrast, CTA head+neck.

## rf_hypertensive_emergency
**HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY.** BP >180/120 + end-organ damage (chest pain, stroke symptoms, pulmonary edema, acute kidney injury, eclampsia). Goal: reduce MAP 20–25% over first hour (NOT more — risk of watershed ischemia). Agents: nicardipine or clevidipine IV drip (safe all comers), labetalol (avoid if bradycardic / severe asthma / cocaine), esmolol if aortic dissection. **Avoid rapid drops.** Asymptomatic severe HTN (urgency) = oral, outpatient — NOT emergency.

## rf_peds_danger_signs
**PEDIATRIC DANGER SIGNS (IMCI).** Any of: (1) unable to drink or breastfeed, (2) vomits everything, (3) convulsions, (4) lethargy / unconscious, (5) severe respiratory distress (chest indrawing, stridor at rest, grunting), (6) cyanosis / SpO2 <92%, (7) pallor, (8) capillary refill >3s. Any danger sign = ADMIT + IV access + empiric abx if <3m or sepsis suspicion. Add: stiff neck / bulging fontanelle = meningitis workup.

## rf_anaphylaxis
**ANAPHYLAXIS.** Likely when acute onset + ≥2 of: (a) skin/mucosal (hives, swelling), (b) respiratory (dyspnea, wheeze, stridor), (c) CV (hypotension, syncope), (d) GI (persistent vomiting, cramping). **IM epinephrine 0.3–0.5 mg (0.01 mg/kg peds) anterolateral thigh IMMEDIATELY** — don't wait for IV. Repeat q5 min if no response. Adjuncts: antihistamines (second-line only), steroids, IVF. Biphasic risk — observe 4–6h minimum.

## rf_dka
**DIABETIC KETOACIDOSIS.** Triad: hyperglycemia (>250) + ketosis (β-hydroxybutyrate >3 or urine ketones) + anion-gap metabolic acidosis (pH <7.3, HCO3 <18). Common in new T1DM or infection / non-adherence. Treatment: (1) IVF — 1L NS then match losses; (2) potassium — check before insulin, hold insulin if K<3.3; (3) insulin — IV drip 0.1 U/kg/h after K checked; (4) search for precipitant (infection, MI, non-adherence).

## rf_pe_massive
**MASSIVE / SUBMASSIVE PE.** Massive = PE + hemodynamic instability (SBP <90 or drop >40). Submassive = PE + RV dysfunction (echo / troponin / BNP). Massive: consider systemic thrombolytics (tPA) if no absolute contraindications. Submassive: heparin bridge → DOAC, consider catheter-directed if deteriorating. Bedside echo: RV dilation, McConnell's sign.

---

## rf_rising_cea_post_resection
**RISING CEA + BLEEDING IN COLORECTAL SURVIVOR.** CEA ≥5 ng/mL or rising trend × 2 consecutive draws in a patient post-curative-intent resection: 80% sensitivity for recurrence before radiographic detection. Add bleeding and time-to-workup compresses: **colonoscopy + CT A/P within 2 weeks, consider PET-CT if imaging equivocal, referral to surgical oncology if local recurrence found**. Source: NCCN Colon Cancer Survivorship, ASCO CEA guideline.

---

## rf_cardiotoxicity_in_survivor
**CHEMOTHERAPY CARDIOTOXICITY RED FLAG IN SURVIVOR.** Any new dyspnea / reduced exercise tolerance / orthopnea / LE edema in a breast / lymphoma survivor with prior anthracycline or trastuzumab = concern for chemo-induced cardiomyopathy. ACTION: ECG, BNP, echocardiogram for LVEF. If LVEF <50% or ≥10% drop from baseline → cardio-oncology referral, consider ACEi + beta blocker per HFSA guideline. Lifelong risk; persists decades post-therapy.

---

## rf_gi_bleed
**ACUTE GI BLEED.** Hematemesis / melena / hematochezia + tachycardia or hypotension = hemodynamically significant. Management: (1) 2 large-bore IV; (2) type & cross 4 units; (3) transfuse if Hgb<7 (or <8 if cardiac); (4) IV PPI bolus + drip if UGIB suspected; (5) octreotide if variceal source; (6) GI consult for endoscopy <24h. NG lavage not routine.

---

## rf_meningitis_adult
**ADULT MENINGITIS.** Classic triad: fever + neck stiffness + altered mental status (only ~45% have all three). Petechial / purpuric rash → meningococcal. Immediate: blood cultures, empiric antibiotics (ceftriaxone + vancomycin; add ampicillin if >50 / immunocompromised for Listeria), dexamethasone 10 mg IV BEFORE or with first abx. LP unless contraindicated (focal deficits, immunocompromised, seizure → CT first). Do NOT delay abx for LP.
